    Causes of Male Infertility and In-Depth Diagnosis Male infertility contributes to a large majority of all cases of infertility due to problems with sperm production, function, or delivery.
    ✅Sperm Disorders : Oligospermia (low sperm count), asthenospermia (sperm motility), or teratospermia (irregularly shaped sperm).
    ✅Varicocele : Dilated veins within the scrotum that may overheat the testicles, diminishing sperm quality and quantity.
    ✅Obstructive Azoospermia : Obstruction of tubes that carry sperm (e.g., vas deferens or epididymis) that hampers their ejaculation.
    ✅Hormonal Imbalances : Deficits or surpluses of hormones such as testosterone, FSH, or LH impacting sperm development.
    ✅Genetic Factors : Some genetic disorders (e.g., Klinefelter syndrome, Y-chromosome microdeletions) may affect fertility.
    ✅Infections : Previous or ongoing infections may impair sperm health or obstruct ducts.
    ✅Ejaculation Disorders : Conditions such as retrograde ejaculation (sperm into the bladder) or ejaculatory dysfunction.
    ✅Lifestyle Factors : Smoking, excessive alcohol consumption, drug use, obesity, stress, and exposure to environmental toxins.

    Proper diagnosis is the basis for successful treatment. Best male infertility specialists in Delhi perform detailed examinations, which typically involve:
    Thorough Medical History and Physical Examination: Including genital examination. Semen Analysis: Several samples tested for sperm count, motility, morphology, and presence of infection.
    Hormone Testing: Blood tests to assess levels of testosterone, FSH, LH, and prolactin. Scrotal Ultrasound: To identify varicoceles or other testicular pathology. Transrectal Ultrasound (TRUS): To examine the prostate and search for obstruction in ejaculatory ducts.
    Genetic Testing: Indicated in very low sperm count or azoospermia to screen for chromosomal defects or Y-chromosome microdeletions. Post-Ejaculation Urinalysis: To screen for retrograde ejaculation. Testicular Biopsy: In a few instances, to distinguish sperm production issues from obstruction.

    Advanced Male Infertility Treatments in Delhi :
    Delhi's top fertility clinics offer a broad range of male infertility treatments, utilizing cutting-edge technologies and experience to deliver individualized care: Lifestyle Changes and Medical Treatment: Modifying aspects such as diet, exercise, smoking cessation, and medication or hormonal replacement for imbalances. Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI): Frequently done in conjunction with IVF, one normal sperm is injected directly into an egg, a very effective treatment for severe male factor infertility (low numbers, poor movement, abnormal form). Surgical Sperm Retrieval (SSR): For individuals with azoospermia (no sperm in the ejaculate) caused by obstruction or production defects, methods such as: PESA (Percutaneous Epididymal Sperm Aspiration) TESA (Testicular Sperm Aspiration) TESE (Testicular Sperm Extraction) Micro-TESE (Microdissection Testicular Sperm Extraction): A newer procedure with improved sperm retrieval rates and reduced tissue damage. Varicocelectomy: Surgical repair of varicocele to enhance sperm quality and quantity. Vasectomy Reversal: Microsurgical vas deferens reconnections for men who previously have been vasectomized. Ejaculatory Duct Obstruction Repair: Surgical repair for ejaculatory duct blockages. Donor Sperm: Where other avenues are not possible, ethical donor sperm schemes open the door to parenthood.
